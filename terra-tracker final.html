What is a Carbon Footprint?

A carbon footprint is the total amount of greenhouse gases — particularly CO₂ — generated by your actions, measured in tonnes CO₂ equivalent (CO₂e) per year.

•Includes direct emissions (driving, flying) and indirect (food production, manufacturing)
•The average global citizen emits about 7 tonnes CO₂e per year
•Sustainable living requires staying under 2 tonnes per person by 2050
🏭
Where Do Emissions Come From?

Global greenhouse gas emissions are spread across several sectors, each contributing differently to climate change and offering different reduction opportunities.

•Energy: 73% of all emissions — electricity, heat, and transport
•Agriculture: 18.4% from livestock, rice, land use change
•Industry: Cement, steel, and chemicals add ~5.2%
•Waste: Landfills and wastewater treatment contribute ~3.2%
🌡️
The Greenhouse Effect

Greenhouse gases trap heat in the Earth's atmosphere, acting like a blanket. Without them the Earth would be too cold for life — but too many cause dangerous warming.

•CO₂ stays in the atmosphere for 300–1,000 years after being released
•Methane (from livestock and landfills) is 80x more potent than CO₂ over 20 years
•N₂O from fertilizers is 265x more potent than CO₂ over 100 years
🌲
Nature-Based Solutions

Forests, wetlands, and oceans are natural carbon sinks that absorb CO₂ from the atmosphere. Protecting and restoring them is critical to meeting climate targets.

•Forests absorb about 2.6 billion tonnes of CO₂ per year globally
•Oceans absorb about 25% of all CO₂ emitted by humans each year
•Rewilding 30% of land could sequester up to 70% of needed CO₂ reductions
